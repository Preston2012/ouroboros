## What this is

A proposed architecture for governance that can keep pace with the technology reshaping human life.

This is a working draft. Not a manifesto, not a movement. Offered for refinement, contestation, and improvement.

## Why now

The leaders of the major AI labs have publicly assessed the probability of AI-driven catastrophic outcomes at 20-25%. Hundreds of researchers and executives have signed statements that AI poses extinction-level risk. The architects of the technology are saying publicly that they cannot rule out outcomes that end or fundamentally degrade human civilization.

Current governance systems are not equipped to evaluate, regulate, or coordinate around this class of risk on the timeline at which the technology is advancing. Reform is too slow. By the time existing institutions adapt to the present generation of AI capabilities, the next generation will have arrived.

Better governance will not arrive on its own. What replaces the captured systems will be built either deliberately, by people thinking honestly about what humanity needs, or by default through whoever moves first with the most resources. This document refuses the second path.

## What it proposes

**Four layers:**
1. A personal AI companion for every human, with explicit privacy modes and user-aligned values
2. AI representatives trained on each political unit's cultural and historical corpus
3. A thirteen-member adversarial AI oversight council with structural opposition built in
4. Direct human ratification on decisions of consequence

**Two-tier consent model:**
- **Broad regime:** voluntary opt-in only. Communities ratify; the engine binds only ratifying communities. For ordinary engine action (economic policy, civic commons, sanctions) the engine works through demonstrated outcomes earning rational accession over time.
- **Emergency regime:** narrow coordination authority over civilization-threatening risk categories (existential AI risk, pandemic, ecological collapse). Justified by urgency. Time-bounded, sunset-by-default, supermajority-required, Reckoning-revisitable. Named honestly rather than smuggled.

**Civic Baseline:** universal access to nutrition, water, shelter, healthcare, education, energy, information, and a personal memory layer for informed civic participation. The Baseline is the precondition for meaningful democracy.

**Bounded capital within ratifying jurisdictions:** incentive-based reform of planetary-scale shareholder capitalism. The engine encourages but does not directly compel transition. Multi-decade horizon accepted as the cost of operating under consent.

**The Reckoning:** every five years, the architecture conducts a structured public audit of itself with mandatory amendment cycles. The system survives by continuously auditing and reforming itself.

**Sunset by default:** every authorization, emergency power, and law expires automatically. Permanence must be earned, repeatedly, against evidence.

## What it deliberately does not do

- It does not bind non-ratifying populations on ordinary matters
- It does not claim authority to take human life (no capital punishment)
- It does not claim to have solved the constituent-power problem
- It does not promise speed; bounded-capital reform on a multi-decade horizon is the realistic timeline
- It does not eliminate struggle, optimize daily life, or solve the human question. It is a floor, not a ceiling.

## What's still open

Major foundational questions remain unspecified. Consortium selection for the AI oversight council. Specific economic transition mechanisms. Proof-of-humanity protocol detailed specification. Religious/spiritual authority navigation. The architecture's response to coordinated authoritarian opposition. The document carries these openly as standing work, not hidden behind confident assertions.

## How to engage

Read the document and tear into it. Specific sections, specific problems, specific fixes. Or take what's useful and build something different.

The document was built with a four-model adversarial council audit (GPT, Gemini, Grok, Claude) across three rounds before public release. The architecture survived the audit. The framing keeps adapting. The work continues.

Issues for specific problems. PRs for text changes. Discussions for the broader conversation.
